About HaitiVoiceNeeds (HVN) Project

This site was developed during the CrisisCampBoston event on January 23, 2010 to address the last mile bottleneck of capturing the needs of the Haitian people after the earthquake. In particular, it avoids the need for computerized data entry systems on the ground, and instead allows ordinary Haitians themselves to state their needs using nothing more than a regular phone call and their voice.

HVN is an Interactive Voice Response (IVR) system that provides a vocalized prompt in the language of the user's choosing (French/Creole or English) for their name, location, contact info, and a statement of their needs, and then captures the individual's needs in their own language. The telephony server then puts the request into a queue (database) for response. Separately, a web page presents the queue of incoming voice requests to volunteer translators who convert the voice recordings into text messages, and put them into an outgoing queue. The outgoing queue then forwards the messages on to electronic response systems like ushahidi.org or WeHaveWeNeed where they can be acted on by rescue and recovery teams on the ground.

You can read more about the development of the project from the HaitiVoiceNeeds wiki page.
